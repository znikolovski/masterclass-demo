Useful information

Planning notes for trekking in Chilean Patagonia — timing, permits, and trail conditions.

Elevation
  • John Gardner Pass: 1,241 m
  • Most camps 200–400 m; cold valley inversions at night
Best time to visit
  • October–April (southern summer)
  • November and March for fewer crowds
Weather
  • Four seasons in a day is normal
  • Sustained wind 60–100 km/h; stake tents well
Culture
  • Spanish primary; English common in refugios
  • Tip guides 10–15%
History
  • Tehuelche routes predate modern trails
  • Park established 1959; UNESCO 1978
Permits
  • Book CONAF entry and campsites online
  • Carry passport copies for ranger checks
